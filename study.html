Study Background: Axolotls

Axolotls, also known as Mexican walking fish, are fascinating aquatic creatures belonging to the salamander family. Here's a brief overview of their unique characteristics and habitat:

Physical Features

Axolotls exhibit neoteny, retaining their aquatic juvenile characteristics throughout their entire life. They have feathery gills, external gill slits, and a wide, flat head. Their limbs are fully developed, and they have a long tail.

Regeneration Abilities

Axolotls are renowned for their exceptional regenerative abilities. They can regrow entire limbs, including bones, muscles, and nerves. This remarkable feature makes them a subject of scientific interest.
